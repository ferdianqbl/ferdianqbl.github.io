It is the most comfortable city I have ever lived in. You will find coolness and beauty in this city. Surrounded by many mountains, many culinary tours, and friendly people. This is My Hometown

Surrounded by Five Mountains

Indonesia has many mountains because it is located between two faults in the earth's plate. There is something unique about Magelang, which is the only city in the world that is surrounded by five mountains at once, namely Mount Merbabu, Merapi, Sumbing, Telomoyo, and Menoreh.

This is an interesting phenomenon in the world and even in Indonesia. Not surprisingly, if Magelang is an area that has a cool atmosphere, this is also the inspiration for the Magelang Military Academy to name it Panca Arga, where Panca means five and Arga means mountain, for one of its residential areas.

Tidar Mountain

The mountain which in legend is known as the "Pakunya Tanah Jawa" is located in the middle of Magelang City and is at an altitude of 503 meters above sea level. At the top of the mountain there are three sacred tombs, the first tomb is the tomb of Shaykh Subakir who is said to be the conqueror of Mount Tidar, the tomb of Kyai Panjang, and the tomb of Kyai Semar who is said to be an important puppet figure.

The Biggest Temple

Borobudur Temple was built in 824 AD by King Samaratungga during the Syailendra dynasty. The construction of Borobudur Temple was completed in 847 AD by Ratu Prabudawardhani, daughter of Samaratungga to glorify Mahayana Buddhism.

Borobudur Temple is located in Borobudur Village, Borobudura District, Magelang Regency, Central Java with an area of 123 x 123 square meters consisting of 504 Buddha statues, 72 overlay stupas, and 1 main stupa.

City of a Million Flowers

The city of Magelang in the Dutch colonial era was also known as Tuin Van Java, or which means the garden or garden of the land of Java.

In addition, many Dutch people used to love to plant flowers, apart from being a symbol of prestige, it was also a way of life. Flower plants also made the Dutch feel at home in Magelang. At that time, flowers that were popular among the Dutch in Magelang were lilies and gladiolus.
